# 🏛️ Loqa Community Governance

## 🌟 Our Vision

Loqa is more than just a voice assistant - it's a **community-driven platform** that embodies our values of privacy, autonomy, and creative freedom. This governance model ensures our community remains inclusive, sustainable, and aligned with our mission.

---

## 🎯 Governance Principles

### 🤝 Community-First Decision Making
- **User and contributor needs** drive development priorities
- **Transparent processes** for all major decisions
- **Inclusive participation** across experience levels and backgrounds
- **Collaborative consensus-building** over top-down mandates

### 🔒 Privacy and Autonomy
- **User privacy rights** are non-negotiable
- **Local-first architecture** remains a core principle
- **No surveillance capitalism** business models
- **Community ownership** of data and direction

### 🌱 Sustainable Growth
- **Long-term thinking** over short-term gains
- **Diverse contributor base** to prevent single points of failure
- **Multiple revenue streams** that align with community values
- **Knowledge sharing** to build organizational resilience

### ⚖️ Fair and Respectful
- **Equal voice** for all community members
- **Merit-based recognition** of contributions
- **Respectful discourse** in all interactions
- **Clear conflict resolution** processes

## 🏗️ Governance Structure

### 🎖️ Community Roles

**👥 Community Members**
- Anyone who participates in Loqa community spaces
- Rights: Participate in discussions, access community resources
- Responsibilities: Follow Code of Conduct, be respectful

**🛠️ Contributors**
- Community members who have made accepted contributions
- Rights: Influence on roadmap, recognition in community
- Responsibilities: Mentor newcomers, maintain quality standards

**🌟 Maintainers**
- Trusted contributors with repository access
- Rights: Merge PRs, release management, moderate discussions
- Responsibilities: Code review, community support, project stewardship

**🏛️ Core Team**
- Long-term project stewards and decision makers
- Rights: Strategic direction, governance changes, conflict resolution
- Responsibilities: Community health, project sustainability, vision alignment

### 🗳️ Decision-Making Process

**Minor Changes** (bug fixes, documentation, small features)
- **Process**: Standard PR review and merge
- **Authority**: Any maintainer can approve
- **Timeline**: 1-3 days for review

**Major Changes** (new features, API changes, dependencies)
- **Process**: RFC (Request for Comments) discussion
- **Authority**: Core team consensus with community input
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks for discussion and consensus

**Breaking Changes** (API changes, architecture shifts)
- **Process**: Formal RFC with migration plan
- **Authority**: Core team unanimous decision
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks with community consultation

**Governance Changes** (this document, Code of Conduct)
- **Process**: Community-wide discussion and feedback
- **Authority**: Core team with broad community support
- **Timeline**: 4-6 weeks for thorough consideration

## 📋 Decision-Making Processes

### 📝 Request for Comments (RFC) Process

**When to Use:**
- New features that affect multiple repositories
- Changes to public APIs or protocols
- Significant architectural decisions
- Policy or process changes

**RFC Workflow:**
1. **Draft**: Create RFC document with problem, solution, alternatives
2. **Discussion**: Open GitHub Discussion for community input (1-2 weeks)
3. **Revision**: Update RFC based on feedback
4. **Decision**: Core team makes final decision with rationale
5. **Implementation**: Approved RFCs tracked to completion

**RFC Template:**
```markdown
# RFC: [Title]

## Summary
Brief description of the proposal

## Motivation
Why is this change needed?

## Detailed Design
Technical specification and implementation approach

## Alternatives Considered
Other approaches and why they were not chosen

## Community Impact
How does this affect users and contributors?

## Implementation Plan
Timeline and milestones for delivery
```

### 🗣️ Community Consultation

**Community Input Methods:**
- **GitHub Discussions**: For ongoing conversations and feedback
- **Discord Community**: Real-time discussion and clarification
- **Community Surveys**: Periodic structured feedback collection
- **Office Hours**: Weekly open sessions for direct dialogue

**Consultation Principles:**
- **Early and Often**: Involve community before final decisions
- **Multiple Channels**: Use various methods to reach different community segments
- **Clear Timeline**: Communicate when input is needed and when decisions will be made
- **Feedback Integration**: Show how community input influenced decisions

## 🤝 Conflict Resolution

### 🛡️ Progressive Resolution Process

**Level 1: Direct Resolution**
- **Scope**: Personal disagreements, minor conflicts
- **Process**: Encourage direct communication between parties
- **Support**: Community Discord mediators available
- **Timeline**: Immediate to 1 week

**Level 2: Maintainer Mediation**
- **Scope**: Technical disagreements, workflow conflicts
- **Process**: Maintainer team facilitates discussion
- **Support**: Structured mediation with clear outcomes
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks

**Level 3: Core Team Intervention**
- **Scope**: Serious violations, persistent conflicts
- **Process**: Core team investigation and resolution
- **Support**: Formal process with documentation
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks depending on complexity

**Level 4: Community Review**
- **Scope**: Governance disputes, appeals
- **Process**: Community-wide discussion and input
- **Support**: Transparent process with community involvement
- **Timeline**: 4-6 weeks for thorough review

### ⚖️ Enforcement Actions

**Available Actions:**
- **Warning**: Formal notice of problematic behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Time-limited removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Complete removal from all community spaces
- **Role Removal**: Loss of maintainer or other privileges

**Appeal Process:**
- All enforcement actions can be appealed to the Core Team
- Appeals must be submitted within 30 days
- Appeal review includes community input when appropriate
- Appeal decisions are final and documented

## 🏆 Recognition & Leadership Development

### 🌟 Contributor Recognition

**Achievement Levels:**
- **New Contributor**: First accepted contribution
- **Regular Contributor**: 5+ accepted contributions
- **Community Champion**: Significant community support activities
- **Technical Leader**: Major technical contributions
- **Project Steward**: Long-term commitment and leadership

**Recognition Methods:**
- **GitHub Achievements**: Automated badges for milestones
- **Community Spotlights**: Monthly contributor highlights
- **Conference Opportunities**: Speaking at events representing Loqa
- **Mentorship Roles**: Guiding new contributors
- **Advisory Positions**: Input on strategic decisions

### 📈 Leadership Pipeline

**Maintainer Path:**
1. **Regular Contributions**: Consistent quality contributions over 3+ months
2. **Community Support**: Active participation in helping other contributors
3. **Technical Expertise**: Demonstrated knowledge in specific areas
4. **Nomination**: Current maintainer nominates with community support
5. **Trial Period**: 3-month provisional maintainer status
6. **Confirmation**: Core team confirms based on performance

**Core Team Path:**
1. **Maintainer Experience**: 6+ months as active maintainer
2. **Leadership Demonstration**: Leading major initiatives or areas
3. **Community Trust**: Strong community support and respect
4. **Vision Alignment**: Clear understanding of project direction
5. **Commitment**: Willingness to make long-term commitment
6. **Consensus Decision**: Unanimous core team agreement

## 💰 Financial Governance

### 💸 Revenue Principles

**Acceptable Revenue Sources:**
- **GitHub Sponsors**: Individual and organizational sponsorships
- **Professional Services**: Consulting, training, custom development
- **Premium Features**: Optional enhancements that don't limit core functionality
- **Grants**: Open source and privacy-focused foundation funding

**Unacceptable Revenue Sources:**
- **User Data Sales**: Any monetization of user data or behavior
- **Advertising**: Third-party advertising or tracking
- **Paywall Core Features**: Basic functionality must remain free
- **Surveillance Partnerships**: Collaboration with surveillance-focused companies

### 💳 Financial Management

**Fund Allocation Priorities:**
1. **Infrastructure Costs**: Hosting, CI/CD, development tools
2. **Community Support**: Events, swag, recognition programs
3. **Development Funding**: Bounties for important features
4. **Legal and Compliance**: Privacy audits, legal reviews
5. **Emergency Fund**: 6-month operational reserve

**Transparency Requirements:**
- **Quarterly Financial Reports**: High-level income and expenditure summaries
- **Annual Budget Planning**: Community input on major financial decisions
- **Expense Tracking**: Clear documentation of how funds are used
- **Conflict of Interest**: Disclosure of any financial relationships

## 🔄 Governance Evolution

### 📊 Regular Reviews

**Annual Governance Review:**
- **Community Survey**: Feedback on governance effectiveness
- **Process Assessment**: Review of decision-making processes
- **Structure Evaluation**: Assessment of roles and responsibilities
- **Improvement Planning**: Roadmap for governance improvements

**Quarterly Health Checks:**
- **Community Health Metrics**: Participation, satisfaction, conflicts
- **Process Efficiency**: Speed and quality of decision-making
- **Leadership Pipeline**: Development of future leaders
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Quality of community information flow

### 🔧 Amendment Process

**Minor Amendments** (clarifications, process improvements)
- **Process**: Core team proposal with 1-week community feedback
- **Approval**: Core team majority decision
- **Implementation**: Immediate

**Major Amendments** (structural changes, new roles)
- **Process**: RFC with 4-week community discussion
- **Approval**: Core team consensus with community support
- **Implementation**: 30-day notice before taking effect

**Foundational Changes** (principles, core values)
- **Process**: Community-wide consultation and discussion
- **Approval**: Core team unanimous decision with strong community support
- **Implementation**: 60-day transition period
